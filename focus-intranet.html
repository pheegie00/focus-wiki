What We Do

Focus Consulting is a software contracting firm serving both government and private clients. We provide services in two ways:

Sub-contracting

We're hired by a prime contractor to augment and support a contract they already have with a client. The prime has primary responsibility for communicating with the client and organizing work.

Direct Staff Augmentation

We serve private/commercial clients by placing our staff on their teams. We directly handle and manage the client relationship, while the client directs the work.

Our clients and prime contracting partners trust us because we:

Onboarding

If you're reading this, you're coming onboard for a new project with Focus Consulting! We're excited to have you and to start making meaningful contributions for our clients.

  • Confirm which project you'll be working on with your manager
  • An onboarding buddy will be assigned to help you ramp up
  • Ensure you have an OSX machine with at least 32GB of memory (Focus will provide if needed)

First Week Goals

  • Learn how to log time for your specific project
  • Complete project-specific onboarding and machine setup

  • Discuss your three month plan with your manager

Three Month Plan

Your manager will put together a three month plan tailored to your project. This plan creates clear expectations for your first few months at Focus with specific goals and objectives. The plan provides guide rails during your onboarding process, not rigid prescriptions.

What We Expect From You

Availability

Our expectations around availability map directly to our value of "Do what you say". Our clients engage us for full time work during regular business hours and our reputation is largely driven by meeting that engagement.

Available means:
  • By your computer where you perform work
  • Able to respond to Slack or email communications
  • Able to attend to any urgent matters

Notification Requirements

Duration Notice Required Action
2 hours or less Right before Notify team via Slack/Teams
2-8 hours Day before Notify team + add to Focus Out of Office calendar
1 day to 1 week Preceding week Notify team + add to calendars
1 week or more 1 month before Notify Focus manager for coverage planning

Note: Regular business hours typically mean 8AM to 6PM EST, adjusted based on your location (e.g., 10:30 AM East Coast / 7:30 AM West Coast).

Work Location

All work must be completed in the continental United States. Working from other locations might result in losing access to client systems.

Timekeeping

Record your time on a daily basis. This is how both you and Focus get paid, and daily tracking is the most reliable way to avoid disruptions in pay.

Status Updates

Provide a brief status update and any blockers in Slack once a week. You'll get a reminder in your project's channel.

Communication Guidelines

As contractors, excellent communication practices are as much part of the job as individual contributions. Our clients often are not technical and rely on us to bring clarity and transparency to complicated concepts.

Client Communication

Key Principles

  • Proactively share status - Focus on providing clarity on work progress. Be transparent when work is blocked or taking longer than expected.
  • Close all loops - Good communication is more than giving information; it's following up, responding, and doing what you say.
  • Formal in public - Keep conversations formal in public channels with key stakeholders to avoid misperception.
  • Practice "take 5 and backtrack" - If conversations become contentious, suggest taking time to reassess before circling back.
  • Admit mistakes - We're all human. Admitting mistakes and committing to learn from them is part of doing the right thing.

Work Communications

  • Provide frequent updates for all in-progress work
  • Keep ticket statuses up to date
  • Update tickets with comments on progress
  • Resolve all comments on ongoing work before moving to something new

Partner Communication (e.g., Nava)

  • Be transparent about availability - Partners rely on our work and need to know our schedules
  • Escalate issues early - Raising issues early builds trust and enables partners to handle them proactively
  • Be respectful - Conform to the norms partners have established in their communication channels

Focus Staff Communication

  • Ask questions publicly - Bring questions to the whole team and document everything for shared knowledge
  • Practice radical candor - Share opinions directly and humbly within our team
  • Escalate early - Let your Focus manager know about problems quickly so they can help resolve them
  • Keep internal communication internal - Use Focus Slack or email for non-client-specific communication
  • Update availability - Keep your availability current in the Focus shared calendar

Communication Best Practices

  • Favor questions over commands - Bring an open mind to all communication
  • Acknowledge others - Recognize and compliment good work
  • Separate work from identity - Critical feedback is not about your value as a person
  • When unsure, loop in your leads

Delivery Approach

Focus has a distinctive approach to delivery that emphasizes outcomes, knowledge transfer, and sustainable practices. Our delivery philosophy is built on proven pillars that guide how we work with clients.

Delivery Pillars

Measure First

We start by understanding current state and establishing metrics before proposing solutions.

Build Second

Solutions are informed by data and real user needs, not assumptions.

Prove Before Scale

We validate approaches with real data before scaling solutions.

Our Philosophy

  • Outcome-based delivery - We focus on results, not just hours worked
  • Knowledge transfer - We build client capacity, not dependency
  • Engineering-first approach - We solve problems through technology, not just adding people
  • Performance-based contracts - We're willing to be measured on outcomes
  • Senior-heavy teams - We invest in experienced practitioners who can deliver independently

Project Practices

  • Two-week sprint cycles with regular releases
  • Bi-weekly retrospectives for continuous improvement
  • Outcome-oriented metrics over utilization tracking
  • Clear definition of done for all work
  • Regular stakeholder communication and demos

Performance & Expectations

Important: Performance expectations are not suggestions. They are a condition of your employment/contract with Focus.

Core Expectations

Staff are expected to:

  • Adhere to all policies and code of conduct
  • Follow working norms and project-specific norms
  • Contribute to our delivery approach
  • Meet performance expectations for their role

Performance Management

Active Support

  • Onboarding driven by 2-3 month plan, with more time for earlier career people
  • Active mentorship for earlier career staff
  • Coaching at earliest signs of inconsistency (not one-offs, but patterns)
  • Most people get one cycle of coaching to improve

Examples of Failure to Meet Expectations

  • No visible progress on tasks over a sprint without asking for help
  • Not providing meaningful status updates
  • Missing multiple team meetings without notice
  • Frequently late to meetings
  • Not adhering to team norms after reminders (draft PRs, daily time tracking, etc.)
  • Team unable to reach you without notification of unavailability

Productivity Impact

Life happens. When your productivity is impacted:

  • Let your manager know when you anticipate or start to see impact, not after
  • Share as much as you're comfortable with
  • Use appropriate time off (sick time, vacation, paid family leave)
  • Discuss if project transfer or vacation might help

Bonus Structure

Focus' bonus structure rewards employees who contribute to company level outcomes. Meeting expectations alone does not result in a bonus but is required for eligibility.

Company Level Outcomes

Builds Our Brand

Yes: Stakeholders know you by name and value your contributions

Strong Yes: Stakeholders trust and depend on your work, likely to recommend Focus

Wins New Business

Yes: Contributed to BD effort with pwin ≥ 75%

Strong Yes: We won work where you contributed positively

Helps Us Scale

Yes: Reduced management overhead through onboarding, mentoring, managing

Strong Yes: Introduced efficiency gains over current processes

How Bonuses Work

  • Input: Bonuses reflect values exhibition and company outcome contributions
  • Amount: Percentage of base salary, up to yearly defined maximum (usually 10-15%)
  • Eligibility: W2 employees at Focus for at least 4 months as of 12/31
  • When: Bonuses paid out in January for the previous year
  • Pool: Decided yearly based on revenues and profit

Important Notes

OS Updates: Do not apply OS upgrades until the all-clear has been given. OSX upgrades can cause issues with tools or machine operation. Wait for verification and specific steps.

Troubleshooting

  • Focus has backup machines available if you're having issues preventing work
  • M1 Macs may have more issues, but all projects have users on them - reach out in project channels
  • For Docker issues, try increasing resources given to the Docker engine
  • Verify you've followed all setup steps correctly

Code of Conduct

Focus is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free environment for everyone. Our code of conduct applies to all Focus spaces, including work on client projects, company events, and online interactions.

Expected Behavior

  • Be respectful and considerate in all interactions
  • Assume good intent from colleagues
  • Practice active listening and open communication
  • Welcome diverse perspectives and experiences
  • Focus on what is best for the team and clients
  • Show empathy towards others
  • Provide and accept constructive feedback gracefully

Unacceptable Behavior

  • Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind
  • Offensive comments related to personal characteristics or identity
  • Unwelcome sexual attention or inappropriate physical contact
  • Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
  • Sustained disruption of discussions or work
  • Publication of private information without consent
  • Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate

Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to your manager or to Meg Greenberg (meg.greenberg@focusconsulting.io) as soon as possible. All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.
